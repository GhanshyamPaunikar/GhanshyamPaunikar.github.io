I build AI that behaves like it's been raised properly — models that are accurate, accountable, and pleasant to be around in production. My work lives at the intersection of research, engineering, and a healthy distrust of hype.

Over the last three years I've led the full arc of ML products — from messy CSVs to 20-stream edge pipelines. I've shipped vision models with 95%+ accuracy, an SRGAN upscaler prototyped for commercial media, and ChatGPT-driven NPCs that lifted player engagement by 45%. I care about clean data, readable code, and the unglamorous work of monitoring what happens after deploy.

Now back in Pune and actively looking for my next role — ideally an AI engineering or data science position where I can own the full arc from prototype to production. Open to remote, hybrid, or on-site across India and internationally.

Work that matters, quietly. Some of the most meaningful hours I've spent weren't in a terminal.

I've volunteered with Spread Smile Foundation, contributing to initiatives focused on education, healthcare, and community development for underprivileged children. The work spanned organising fundraising events, running tutoring sessions, and helping with awareness drives — the kind of work that asks for patience more than cleverness.

It taught me something engineering rarely does: that empathy and consistency compound harder than any metric, and that showing up — again and again — is its own kind of skill.

Recently I've started picking up a camera. Photography is new territory — learning to see before shooting, to find the frame inside the noise. The same instinct that makes a good data visualisation, I think, makes a good photograph: knowing what to leave out.
